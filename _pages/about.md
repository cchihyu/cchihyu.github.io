I am a PhD student in Mathematics at [Imperial College London](https://www.imperial.ac.uk), supervised by [Prof. Kelly Zhang](https://kellywzhang.github.io) and co-supervised by Prof. Andrew Duncan. My research lies at the intersection of statistics and machine learning, with interests in sequential decision making, Bayesian optimization, and statistical learning.

Before joining Imperial, I earned an MSc in Applied Statistics from the [University of Michigan, Ann Arbor](https://lsa.umich.edu/stats) and a BSc in Mathematics from [National Tsing Hua University](https://www.math.nthu.edu.tw/index.php). I have also worked with Dr. Ming-Chung Chang at the [Institute of Statistical Science, Academia Sinica](https://www.stat.sinica.edu.tw/eng/).

## Research

My current and recent work focuses on methods for learning and decision making from limited or distributed data, including:

- sequential decision making and bandit inference;
- Bayesian optimization with human or model-in-the-loop feedback;
- federated and collaborative statistical learning;
- experimental design for black-box models.

[Read more about my research](/research/).
