Bootstrap aggregation, or bagging, is a fundamental ensemble learning technique for improving predictive performance. Its effectiveness depends strongly on how correlations among aggregated models are managed.

This work studies bootstrap aggregation for regression through a generalized least squares perspective. By accounting for dependence among base learners, the method aims to improve aggregation efficiency and predictive accuracy. The paper develops the statistical motivation for this approach and evaluates its performance in regression settings.
